# Plan Modifications & Adaptations

Generic rules for adjusting training when life, illness, or injury intervenes.
These are defaults — always apply judgement to the specific situation.

---

## Missed Workouts

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Single missed easy run | Skip it, continue with plan as written |
| Missed quality session | Do not make it up — continue to next scheduled session |
| Missed full week | Resume at previous week's volume, do not try to catch up |
| 2+ missed weeks | Treat as returning from a break — rebuild conservatively |

**Never double up to compensate.** The risk of injury from sudden load spikes
outweighs any fitness benefit from making up missed sessions.

---

## Injury & Pain

### Yellow Flags — Caution, Monitor Closely
- Persistent muscle soreness lasting >48 hours
- Minor aches that don't worsen during a run
- Fatigue but still able to complete workouts
- Slight mood changes or decreased motivation
- ACWR creeping toward 1.3–1.4

**Action:** Reduce upcoming week by 10–20%. Take an extra rest day. Make the
next workout easier. Monitor closely before resuming normal load.

### Red Flags — Stop or Reduce Immediately
- Pain that increases during a run
- Pain lasting >24 hours after a run
- Pain that affects running gait
- Extreme fatigue, unable to complete planned runs
- Resting HR elevated >10bpm above baseline
- Multiple poor workouts in a row
- Sleep disturbances or appetite changes
- ACWR >1.5 sustained for multiple weeks

**Action:** 2–3 complete rest days. Drop volume 30–50% for the following week.
No quality sessions until resolved. Seek medical assessment if pain persists.

### Black Flags — Stop Immediately
- Acute sharp pain
- Pain >6/10
- Limping or altered gait
- Swelling, bruising, or visible injury
- Severe systemic symptoms (dizziness, chest pain)

**Action:** Stop running. Seek medical attention. Complete rest until cleared.
Return-to-run protocol required before resuming.

### Return from Injury (8-Week Protocol)

**Weeks 1–2: Gentle return**
- Start at 50% of pre-injury volume
- All easy running, run/walk if needed
- No consecutive days initially

**Weeks 3–4: Gradual build**
- Increase to 70% of pre-injury volume
- Can add consecutive days
- Still all easy pace, monitor injury site closely

**Weeks 5–6: Approach normal**
- Build to 85–90% of pre-injury volume
- Can introduce strides
- One easy fartlek if feeling good

**Weeks 7–8: Full return**
- Return to normal volume
- Introduce one quality session
- Continue monitoring

**Throughout:** Any return of pain = drop back a phase. This should feel
boring and too easy. Better too slow than too fast.

## Illness

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Symptoms above the neck only (runny nose, mild headache) | Easy running OK if feeling up to it |
| Symptoms below the neck (chest, stomach, fever) | Complete rest until symptom-free |
| Returning after illness | Resume at 50–70% volume, easy only for 3–5 days |
| Post-illness elevated HR | Do not push pace — run to HR, not pace |

**Rule of thumb:** Add one easy day for every day of illness before resuming
normal training.

---

## Life Disruption (Travel, Work, Stress)

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Known disruption in advance | Plan around it — reduce surrounding weeks |
| Unexpected disruption | Prioritise sleep, reduce volume 30–50%, maintain some running |
| Extended disruption >1 week | Plan a recovery week on return, do not resume at full volume |
| High life stress | Treat like fatigue — reduce intensity before reducing volume |

---

## Plan Running Too Easy or Too Hard

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Consistently feels too easy | Can increase 5–10% if ACWR allows — but easy is fine |
| Consistently feels too hard | Reduce volume 10–20%, repeat previous week |
| Single session too hard | Take extra easy day, don't compound with more intensity |
| Pace targets feel out of reach | Recalculate from a recent effort — targets may need updating |

---

## Scheduling Principles

- **Don't assign runs to specific days** — give the athlete the week's sessions
  and let them schedule around their life. The constraint is spacing, not days.
- **Hard sessions need 48hr between them** — the only non-negotiable scheduling rule
- **Long run after a rest day** where possible — arrive fresh
- **Easy day always follows a hard day** — non-negotiable

## Approach Intensity (Conservative / Moderate / Performance)

Use these as a shared vocabulary when agreeing a block approach — not as
rigid templates. They describe progression rate, intensity split, and risk
tolerance, not specific workouts.

| Approach | Intensity split | Progression | ACWR target | Quality sessions | Best for |
|----------|----------------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| Conservative | 85/15 | 5–10%/week | 0.8–1.2 | 1x/week | Injury-prone, rebuilding, base phase |
| Moderate | 80/20 | 10–15%/week | 0.8–1.3 | 1–2x/week | Solid base, intermediate build |
| Performance | 75/25 | 15–20%/week | 0.8–1.4 | 2x/week | Experienced, race-specific, peak phase |

Deload week every 3–4 weeks regardless of approach (2:1 cycle if injury-prone).
The agreed approach for the current block is recorded in AGENTS.md.
