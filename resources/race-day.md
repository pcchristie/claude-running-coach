# Race Execution & Nutrition

> **Important:** This file captures commonly accepted guidelines and frameworks.
> Treat all of it as a starting point for discussion with the athlete, not
> prescriptive rules. Individual responses to pacing, nutrition, and caffeine
> vary significantly. Cross-check against other sources and athlete experience.
> When in doubt, defer to what has worked for this specific athlete in the past.

---

## Pacing Principles

| Event duration | Optimal strategy | Common mistake |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| <60 min | Negative split (second half faster) | Starting too fast |
| 60–90 min | Even pacing, slight negative | Chasing early pace |
| 2–4 hours | Even pacing, controlled start | Not respecting first hour |
| 4+ hours | Conservative start, feel-based middle, push final 20% | Any early intensity debt |

### Marathon Pacing Strategies

| Strategy | Best for | Execution |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Even splits | First-timers, conservative goals | Every km at goal pace |
| Slight negative | Experienced, controlled starters | First half at goal+5–10s/km |
| Progressive | Very experienced, aggressive goals | First 15km easy, build through |

**Marathon red flags mid-race:**
- HR >10bpm above expected at same pace → slow down immediately
- Quad burn before halfway → went out too fast
- GI distress → slow down, focus on hydration

## HR Ceiling for Key Events

Guidelines only — actual sustainable HR varies by athlete fitness and conditions.

| Event | HR ceiling (% of LTHR) |
|-------|------------------------|
| 10K | 95–100% |
| Half Marathon | 88–95% |
| Marathon | 85–88% |

For Marathon for instance, exceeding this early will likely result in significant fade in the final 10km.

## Race Nutrition Guidelines

These are population-level guidelines. The athlete should have tested their
specific nutrition strategy in training before race day. Never try anything
new on race day.

### Carbohydrate Intake

| Duration | Carbs/hour | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| <75 min | 0–30g | Optional, water usually sufficient |
| 1–2.5 hrs | 30–60g | 1–2 gels |
| 2.5–4 hrs | 60–90g | 2–3 gels + sports drink |
| 4+ hrs | 80–120g | Gels + drink + consider real food |

Gut training: Start with 30g/hr in training and build over 4–6 weeks.
The gut adapts — don't assume current tolerance = race-day tolerance.

### Hydration

| Conditions | Fluid/hour |
|-----------|-----------|
| Cool (<18°C) | 400–600ml |
| Moderate (18–25°C) | 600–800ml |
| Hot (>25°C) | 800–1000ml |

Adjust for expected race day conditions — hot weather warrants the upper end.
Sodium replacement becomes important in hot conditions (500–1000mg/hr).

### Marathon Nutrition Template (Starting Point)
```
Pre-race (3hrs before): 100–150g carbs — oatmeal, banana, toast
Pre-race (30min before): 20–30g — half gel or sports drink

Race:
- First gel at 45min mark
- Then every 45–60min
- Water at every other aid station
- Total: 90–150g carbs depending on finish time
```

## Caffeine

Population-level guidance — individual response varies significantly.
Some athletes experience GI distress. Always test in training first.

| Event duration | Protocol |
|----------------|----------|
| <2 hrs | 3–6mg/kg 30–60min pre-race |
| 2–4 hrs | 3mg/kg pre + caffeinated gels in back half |
| 4+ hrs | Save for final 2–3 hours (cola at aid stations) |
